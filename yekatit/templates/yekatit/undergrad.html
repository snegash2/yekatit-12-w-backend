Welcome to the Undergraduate Medicine Program at Yekatit 12 Hospital Medical College. Our program is designed to provide aspiring medical students with a comprehensive and rigorous education in the field of medicine. We offer a dynamic curriculum, experienced faculty, and state-of-the-art facilities to prepare students for a successful career in medicine.

The following are the seven domains of competencies in undergraduate medicine program:
  1. Professional Values, Attitudes, Behavior and Ethics
  2. Scientific Foundation of Medicine
  3. Communication Skills
  4. Clinical Skills,
  5. Population Health and Health Systems,
  6. Management of Information,
  7. Critical Thinking and Research and Practice-Based Improvement

Program Overview: The Undergraduate Medicine Program is a full-time, five-year program that combines classroom learning, practical training, and clinical experiences. Our curriculum is carefully structured to provide a strong foundation in the basic sciences, clinical skills, and professional development. We aim to produce competent and compassionate physicians who are equipped to meet the healthcare needs of our community and beyond.

Pre-Clinical Years: The first two years of the program, known as the pre-clinical years, focus on building a solid understanding of the basic medical sciences. Students learn subjects such as anatomy, physiology, biochemistry, pharmacology, pathology, microbiology, and behavioral sciences. They engage in interactive lectures, small group discussions, laboratory sessions, and hands-on training in our well-equipped facilities.

Clinical Years: The final three years of the program, known as the clinical years, provide students with valuable clinical experiences in various specialties. Students rotate through different clinical departments, including internal medicine, surgery, pediatrics, obstetrics and gynecology, psychiatry, and others. Under the guidance of experienced faculty and preceptors, students actively participate in patient care, physical examinations, diagnostic procedures, and treatment planning. They develop clinical skills, professionalism, and effective communication with patients and healthcare teams.

Elective Rotations: In addition to the core clinical rotations, students have the opportunity to choose elective rotations in specific areas of interest. These elective rotations allow students to explore different medical specialties, gain insight into subspecialties, and further tailor their education to their career goals. Students receive mentorship and guidance from faculty members to make informed decisions about their elective rotations.

Research and Scholarly Activities: We encourage students to engage in research and scholarly activities during their undergraduate years. Our program provides opportunities to participate in research projects, present findings at conferences, and publish in scientific journals. Students have access to research mentors, resources, and a supportive research environment to nurture their academic growth.

Community Engagement and Outreach: We believe in the importance of community engagement and service. Our program encourages students to participate in community health initiatives, volunteer work, and outreach programs. These experiences provide students with a broader perspective on healthcare, cultural competency, and the social determinants of health.

Professional Development: Our program is dedicated to nurturing the professional development of our students. We provide guidance in areas such as medical ethics, communication skills, leadership, and lifelong learning. Students receive mentorship from faculty members and have access to career counseling services to support their personal and professional growth.

Examinations and Assessment: Throughout the program, students undergo regular assessments, including written exams, practical exams, clinical evaluations, and OSCEs (Objective Structured Clinical Examinations). These assessments ensure that students are progressing effectively and acquiring the necessary knowledge and skills to become competent physicians.

Certification and Career Pathways: Upon successful completion of the Undergraduate Medicine Program, students are awarded a medical degree. Graduates are eligible to take the licensing examinations required to practice medicine. They can pursue various career pathways, including residency training in different medical specialties, academic medicine, research positions, public health, healthcare administration, and more.

We are proud of our Undergraduate Medicine Program at Yekatit 12 Hospital Medical College. We invite enthusiastic and driven individuals to join us on this transformative journey of becoming skilled and compassionate physicians who make a positive impact on the health and well-being of individuals and communities.
